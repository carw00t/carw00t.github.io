THE WHIMSICAL A.I. PROJECT:
I'M A WRITER

A project to teach Artificial Intelligence to write short stories

This project was started in 2012 by a group of Japanese scientists led by Dr. Hitoshi Matsubara of Future University Hakodate. The team uses Hoshi’s more than 1,000 short stories as data, and uses Hoshi's essays on how to write stories as a guidance.

The team was able to create several short stories in 2015 partly using the A.I. They entered a couple of them to the third annual Hoshi Awards and one of them made the first round of preliminary selection. The team hopes to increase the proportion of the use of A.I. and someday let A.I. write stories on its own.

The name The Whimsical A.I. Project comes from Hoshi’s famous science fiction The Whimsical Robot, and I’m a Writer comes from Hoshi’s other famous work I’m a Hit Woman.
